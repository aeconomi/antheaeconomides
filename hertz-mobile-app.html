Hertz Mobile App

Project Summary

The old Hertz app was outdated, crashed frequently, and required development in order to update content. In partnering with IBM, our team worked with the Marketing, UX, and Creative Services teams to deliver the new Hertz Mobile App. The new Hertz app gives customers a faster rental experience, allowing users to quickly login with touch ID, manage their rewards, and find nearby locations in an updated user interface. My role was to help provide and maintain the content, and design templates that could be used to collaborate with brand and development teams. I also played a role in marketing the launch of the app by building landing pages and posting banners and videos on the website to help drive awareness.

Team Members & Collaborators

IBM Garage, UX, Loyalty, Analytics team, and Marketing Stakeholders

Tools

Photoshop, Adobe XD, Adobe Experience Manager, Excel

Analysis

Giving our customers better speed and convenience during their rental journey was a primary focus for the new app. One of the biggest problems customers had with the old app was that it either froze, crashed, or was slow to load.


Some of the top uses of the old Hertz app was to:


1. Book a Reservation
2. Modify/Cancel Reservations
3. Checking Loyalty Information & Point Balance
4. Update Account Information
5. Find Return Location Directions


Since the previous app redirected customers to the website for three of the five top uses, it was important that we included all of these uses in the new mobile app. We also wanted the app to help answer some of the top questions asked by customers:


  • Where’s my reservation number?
  • How many points do I have?
  • What is my Gold Plus Rewards account number?
  • Looking to find receipts?
  • Need to update credit card?
  • Find parking services near me?
  • What is returning address of location?
  • Looking to change arrival time?
  • Looking to bypass vehicle counter?



The plan was to launch with these core features but to continue to iterate to deliver new functionality that would help us surpass our competitors.

Research and Segmentation insights gathered by Mizzouri for Hertz in 2018

As part of the content strategy we needed to appeal to these 3 different segments but also reduce unnecessary content and keep things simple and easy to digest. Including a fleet guide with high-res vehicle images would appeal to Car Enthusiasts. Promotions featuring vacation getaways and special offers would attract Deal Seekers. Travel tips and Gold Plus Rewards benefits would appeal to Quality Confidants in addition to offering essential information about their rental car journey.

Plan

Since the previous app had been very limiting in terms of maintenance and updates- all updates had to be done by developers- it was our goal to have the CMS host as much content as possible, enabling our team to swap out imagery, text, or CTA’s as marketing initiatives and priorities changed.

Design

The design and layout was built by the IBM development team along with our in-house UX and design teams but it was my job to create imagery templates that could be used to ensure there was a consistent visual appearance and brand reinforcement throughout the app.


It was important that the imagery and graphics displayed in the app shared similar characteristics with the website and aligned with the new Brand Guidelines. Hertz rebranded in 2017 and the new look and feel was black and white with a pop of yellow.


While this color scheme was eye-catching and a great opportunity for brand reinforcement, it made it tough to find imagery that had high enough contrast to pass web accessibility standards when overlaid with text. We had to be very careful we were adhering to color-blind standards. We also needed to follow the new brand guidelines which had changed from using dark photography with heavy black accents to incorporating light and airy images with purposeful pops of yellow.


Overall we needed to solve two problems in the imagery designs:


1. Make the Text Readable
2. Minimize Dark Imagery


We came up with 3 different image treatments solutions to present to brand.


1. Overlay entire image
2. Solid color block behind text
3. Scrim Overlay


In the end, the scrim overlay was chosen because it was a subtle enough design change that didn’t affect the integrity of the image while also improving text readability.


For photography, three categories reflected the Hertz visual identity: Vehicle, Business and Leisure. For the image selections, multiple options were presented to the Brand team (iterations shown below). The selections were chosen based on those that communicated an elevated and effortless travel experience.

To create the templates, I used a combination of Adobe XD and Indesign.

In order to make sure the images looked good on both Android and iOS, I created templates that could be used in our image export process. These ensured images were positioned and cropped in the right places on both operating systems.

Strategy and Implementation

Goal #1:
Customer Acquisition & Retention


In order to attract new loyalty members in the app, one of our goals was to include fast track enrollment within the reservation flow. This made it easier for new renters to sign-up for our loyalty program as it created an account using their information entered in the reservation process.


Content cards would also give us increased personalization and the opportunity to target different segments (i.e. member vs non-member) with Adobe Target integration in the future. [insert picture of loyalty content card]

Goal #2: Increase Reserved Revenue


In order to improve revenue and encourage bookings on the mobile app we needed to make the rental process and log-in as easy as possible. For our customers convenience, the below features were included in development:


  • “Pay with points” in the reservation flow
  • Bio-metric log in (Facial & Touch)
  • Closest location finder
  • Location search history

Goal #3 Improve Revenue Per Rental


One of the top ways to improve the amount of revenue per rental was to increase ancillary sales (add-ons such as purchasing fuel ahead of time, better insurance coverage, etc).


Customer friendly messaging, premium photography & images of high-end fleet helped to provide an elevated and effortless booking process and appeal to the car enthusiast and quality confidant customer segments.


Goal #4 Help the Customer


We wanted the new app to be helpful and convenient for our customers and add functionality that would address the top pain points customers had on the old app. It was important to add a section where customers could view & update profile information, view past rental history and find their receipts.


Goal #5 Support Operations


The last goal was to reduce the amount of app crashes and reduce load on developers by enabling our team to update the content.


Since the previous app had been very limiting in terms of maintenance and updates- all updates had to be done by developers- it was our goal to have the CMS host as much content as possible, enabling our team to swap out imagery, text, or CTA’s as marketing initiatives and priorities changed. We would become the go-to contacts for updates and maintenance on the app.

Design for the future

Since the previous app had been very limiting in terms of maintenance and updates- all updates had to be done by developers- it was our goal to have the CMS host as much content as possible on the new app, enabling our team to swap out imagery, text, or CTA’s as marketing initiatives and priorities changed.


We also wanted to make updates to style and imagery easier. To do this, developers coded the app so that most of the imagery pulled from the CMS, and could be swapped out as needed. We also found that adding gradient overlays directly on the images (vs hard coding these in the app) would make it easier for us to update the look and feel in case our visual rulebook changed. Though we may have lost some consistency and speed of updating these assets, it gave us more flexibility in the future.

Summary

The new Hertz Mobile App achieved a 4.8 out 5 star rating on the App Store, compared to the previous 3.8 stars on the old app. The redesign also led to a 32% increase in monthly mobile app users and a 50% increase in sessions YOY. We also saw a positive impact in engagement, increasing screens/session by 84%.


Since launch, the mobile app team has continued to iterate and add new functionality. As a content team, we were able to swap out marketing banners as new promotions arose, update special offers in the deals section, and make updates to terms and conditions to communicate changes in policies. The new app had a lot more flexibility and enabled marketing to make direct updates- reducing time and expenses associated with developer involvement.


I was grateful to be a part of the mobile app development project as it was interesting to learn what takes place behind the scenes- from updating string files to differences in operating systems. I also got a sense of what goes into approvals and the best way to report updates to leadership.
