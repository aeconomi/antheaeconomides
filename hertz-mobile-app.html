Hertz Mobile App

Project Summary

The old Hertz app was outdated, crashed frequently, and required development in order to update content. In partnering with IBM, our team worked with the Marketing, UX, and Creative Services teams to deliver the new Hertz Mobile App. The new Hertz app gives customers a faster rental experience, allowing users to login with touch ID, manage their rewards, and find nearby locations in an updated user interface. My role was to help provide the content and design templates that could be used to collaborate with the brand and development teams. I also played a role in marketing the launch of the app by building landing pages and posting banners and videos on the website to help drive awareness.

Team Members & Collaborators

IBM Garage, UX, Loyalty, Analytics team, and Marketing Stakeholders

Tools

Photoshop, Adobe XD, Adobe Experience Manager, Excel

Analysis

Giving our customers better speed and convenience during their rental journey was a primary focus for the new app. One of the biggest problems customers had with the old app was that it either froze, crashed, or was slow to load.


Some of the top uses of the old Hertz app was to:


  1. Book a Reservation
  2. Modify/Cancel Reservations (redirected customers to .com)
  3. Checking Loyalty Information & Point Balance (redirected customers to .com)
  4. Update Account Information (redirected customers to .com)
  5. Find Return Location Directions

Since the previous app redirected customers to the website for three of the five top uses, it was important that we included all of these uses in the new mobile app. We also wanted the app to address the top questions asked by customers by adding new features:


  • Where is my reservation number?
    Include a section where members can easily locate their reservation number for upcoming rentals.
  • How many points do I have?
  • What is my Gold Plus Rewards account number?
    Provide a personalized experience that displays member name, number, status and point balance in one view.
  • Where can I find my receipt?
    Include a section where customers can find and view rental receipts for drop-off confirmation.
  • Where can I update my credit card?
    Include the ability for customers to modify their profile, and preferences on coverages and payment options
  • Are there any nearby parking services?
    Include Parking Service Integration with Spot Hero.
  • What is the address of my return location?
    Provide location details including phone number, hours of operations, or address for directions back to branch.
  • Can I change the time of my arrival?
    Make it easy to view details of current reservation for return times or location information.


The plan was to launch with these core features but to continue to iterate to deliver new functionality that would help us surpass our competitors.

Old Hertz App

Plan

Since the previous app had been very limiting in terms of maintenance and updates- all updates had to be done by developers- it was our goal to have the CMS host as much content as possible, enabling our team to swap out imagery, text, or CTA’s as marketing initiatives and priorities changed.

Design

The design and layout was built by the IBM development team along with our in-house UX and design teams but it was my job to create imagery templates that could be used to ensure there was a consistent visual appearance and brand reinforcement throughout the app.


It was important that the imagery and graphics displayed in the app shared similar characteristics with the website and aligned with the new Brand Guidelines. Hertz rebranded in 2017 and the new look was modernized with bright airy photogrophy and pops of yellow.


While this color scheme was eye-catching and a great opportunity for brand reinforcement, it made it tough to find imagery that had high enough contrast to pass web accessibility standards when overlaid with text. We had to be very careful we were adhering to color-blind standards.

Design for the future

Since the previous app had been very limiting in terms of maintenance and updates- all updates had to be done by developers- it was our goal to have the CMS host as much content as possible on the new app, enabling our team to swap out imagery, text, or CTA’s as marketing initiatives and priorities changed.


We also wanted to make updates to style and imagery easier. To do this, developers coded the app so that most of the imagery pulled from the CMS, and could be swapped out as needed. We also found that adding gradient overlays directly on the images (vs hard coding these in the app) would make it easier for us to update the look and feel in case our visual rulebook changed. Though we may have lost some consistency and speed of updating these assets, it gave us more flexibility in the future.

Summary


Since launch, the mobile app team has continued to iterate and add new functionality. As a content team, we were able to swap out marketing banners as new promotions arose, update special offers in the deals section, and make updates to terms and conditions to communicate changes in policies. The new app had a lot more flexibility and enabled marketing to make direct updates- reducing time and expenses associated with developer involvement.


I was grateful to be a part of the mobile app development project as it was interesting to learn about all the work that goes into app development- from updating string files to differences in operating systems. I also got a sense of what goes into approvals and the best way to report updates to leadership.


The new Hertz Mobile App achieved a 4.8 out 5 star rating on the App Store, compared to the previous 3.8 stars on the old app. The redesign also led to a 32% increase in monthly mobile app users and a 50% increase in sessions YOY. We also saw a positive impact in engagement, increasing screens/session by 84%.
