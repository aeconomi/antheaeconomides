Corporate Responsibility

Project Summary

The old corporate responsibility page had very little content and was not mobile-responsive. A press release and annual report section was necessary but we also wanted to make the page inviting. After the UX team created the designs, I developed the page using HTML, CSS, and javascript.

Team Members & Collaborators

UX and Corporate Responsibility Teams

Tools

Photoshop, Adobe XD, Adobe Dreamweaver

Analysis

Hertz was expanding the scope of it's corporate responsibility programs to make a positive impact on the environment, its employees, and the sustainability of its fleet. While the company was taking active steps towards ramping up the program, communication of these efforts was lacking. The previous landing page was very sparse, with two links to fuel-efficient cars and three Corporate Responsibility reports.

Plan

The Hertz Corporate Responsibility team contacted our UX team for help designing a new landing page that would better communicate Hertz's commitment towards corporate responsibility. The UX team wanted to include different sections attributed to the program, relevant to the page's primary visitors: travel conscious customers, potential employees, investors, and other proponents of corporate responsibility. My goal was to create a responsive landing page that loaded quickly and would be easy to maintain since it would receive ongoing updates as new press releases and reports were published.

Design & Development





Summary

This was an exploratory project and the stakeholders ended up wanting to take the project in a different direction, ultimately deciding to turn the landing page into a micro site for corporate responsibility. The project was put on hold right after we finished design and development so we did not get to push it live to see the impact of our changes.
